The World's Smallest GPS Tracker: Size & Weight Comparison

15 Grams. Real GPS. 20km Range. No Monthly Fee.

Size and weight matter more in GPS trackers than most buyers realize — until they try to mount one on a racing drone, attach it to a small dog's collar, or pack it into an ultralight hiking kit. Most "compact" GPS trackers are still 30–150 grams, the size of a lighter or small matchbox.

Loko Air weighs 15g. That is lighter than a standard AAA battery. Despite this, it achieves 20km LoRa radio range, up to 1 year battery life, and real GPS/GLONASS/Galileo positioning — capabilities that competing trackers 10x its weight cannot match.

This page compares the smallest GPS trackers on the market by the numbers that actually matter: weight, size, range, battery, and cost.

Why GPS Tracker Size and Weight Matter

For many use cases, a tracker that is too heavy or too bulky is simply not usable — regardless of how good its tracking is:

  • Drones: Every gram of payload directly reduces flight time. A 50g tracker on a 250g micro drone represents 20% of the total mass. FPV racing drones are tuned to gram-level precision. At 15g, Loko Air adds negligible weight and does not require rebalancing.
  • Small dogs and cats: Vets recommend pet accessories weigh no more than 10% of the animal's body weight. A 3kg toy poodle should not carry more than 300g on its collar. A 50g GPS tracker is already a significant fraction of that. At 15g, Loko Air is appropriate for dogs down to ~1kg.
  • Luggage and valuables: A discrete tracker hidden in a bag needs to be small enough not to create a visible bulge or add notable weight to the item being tracked.
  • Ultralight hiking: Backpackers on gram-counting trips scrutinize every piece of kit. A 15g tracker with up to 1-year battery is a completely different proposition from a 50g cellular tracker that needs recharging every 3 days.
  • Racing pigeon tracking: Competition rules often limit attachment weight to 5–10% of bird weight. For birds weighing 300–400g, a 15g tracker is essential.

What Loko Achieves at 15 Grams

The engineering challenge of building a tracker this small with these capabilities is significant. Here is what is packed into Loko Air's tiny form factor:

  • Multi-constellation GPS receiver: Simultaneous GPS, GLONASS, and Galileo reception for fast, accurate fixes in varied conditions
  • LoRa radio transmitter: Achieves 20km range on 14 dBm output power using a chip antenna — no large external antenna required
  • Low-power microcontroller: Manages GPS acquisition, LoRa scheduling, and power management with a firmware optimized for minimum energy consumption
  • Battery: Small rechargeable LiPo cell, rechargeable via USB, lasting up to 1-years at normal use intervals
  • Open source design: Every component was selected for optimal power-to-size ratio, with the full BOM and schematics publicly available

The key to achieving this size is LoRa radio. A cellular modem requires much more complex radio circuitry and higher power amplification to reach cell towers. LoRa's spread-spectrum technology achieves extraordinary range with minimal hardware and power — which is why Loko can be so small while outranging most cellular trackers in off-grid environments.

Use Cases Where Small Size Is Critical

Beyond the obvious weight advantage, a small form factor opens up tracking possibilities that simply are not practical with bulkier devices:

  • FPV drone recovery: When a long-range FPV drone goes out of control and lands in an unknown location, you need to find it. Loko Air at 15g fits on most drones without affecting the center of gravity, and its 20km LoRa range covers even the most ambitious flights. Cellular trackers fail because there may be no signal at the landing site.
  • Exotic pet tracking: Large parrots, ferrets, and other pets that can escape through small gaps need small trackers. Under 15g is the threshold for comfortable attachment to a harness on mid-sized exotic pets.
  • Backpack hidden placement: A tracker hidden inside a backpack for theft recovery needs to be small enough to conceal in a seam, pocket lining, or frame channel. Under 15g fits practically anywhere.
  • Competitive cycling: Tracking a bicycle in a race or preventing theft requires a tracker small enough to hide inside a handlebar, seat tube, or under a saddle. Every gram adds up on a racing bike.
  • Marine buoy and equipment marking: Attaching a tracker to a fishing net, lobster pot, or small buoy — where waterproof attachment hardware may add weight — benefits from the lightest possible base device.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Loko Air GPS tracker weigh?

Loko Air weighs 15g. This makes it one of the lightest GPS trackers with real-time long-range tracking available. The low weight is critical for mounting on drones, small animals, or lightweight gear without affecting performance or causing discomfort.

Why does GPS tracker weight matter?

Weight matters enormously in several tracking contexts. FAA drone regulations limit payload weight, and a heavy tracker can exceed allowable limits. Small dogs should not carry more than 10% of their body weight on a collar — a 3kg dog should not have a 50g tracker. Endurance athletes and ultralight hikers count every gram. And on a racing drone or fixed-wing aircraft, even a few extra grams affect flight dynamics significantly.

How does Loko achieve such a small size with 20km range?

The key is LoRa radio technology. Cellular modems require large antennas and high-power amplifiers to reach cell towers miles away. LoRa achieves 20km range with a tiny chip antenna and just 14 dBm transmit power, because LoRa's spread-spectrum modulation is extremely efficient at extracting signals from noise. The GPS chip, LoRa radio, microcontroller, and battery all fit in a package 15g.

Can Loko Air be used on micro drones?

Yes. At 15g, Loko Air is light enough to mount on most consumer and semi-professional drones without significantly affecting flight time or stability. It can be secured with double-sided foam tape, zip ties, or a custom 3D-printed mount. Many drone pilots use Loko to recover aircraft that have flown out of visual range or crashed in difficult terrain.

Is Loko safe to put on a small dog or cat?

Yes. At 15g, Loko Air is appropriate for dogs over approximately 1kg (where the 10% body weight rule still comfortably allows it). It attaches to a collar or harness and is compact enough not to snag on vegetation or restrict movement. For very small cats or toy breeds, verify the total attachment weight including the mounting method.
